About Infectious Diseases

Infectious diseases are caused by pathogenic microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. These diseases can spread directly or indirectly from one person to another, through contaminated surfaces, food, water, or insect bites.

Types of Infectious Diseases & Examples

  • Bacterial Infections: Tuberculosis (TB), Pneumonia, Meningitis.
  • Viral Infections: COVID-19, Influenza (Flu), HIV/AIDS, Rabies.
  • Fungal Infections: Candidiasis, Aspergillosis.
  • Parasitic Infections: Malaria, Toxoplasmosis.

Transmission Methods

  • Airborne: TB, COVID-19, Flu.
  • Direct Contact: HIV, Hepatitis B, Rabies.
  • Vector-borne: Malaria (mosquitoes), Lyme disease (ticks).
  • Food & Waterborne: Cholera, Salmonella, Hepatitis A.

Prevention & Control

  • Vaccination: Flu, COVID-19, Hepatitis, MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella).
  • Hygiene & Sanitation: Washing hands, disinfecting surfaces.
  • Vector Control: Mosquito nets, insect repellents.
  • Safe Food & Water Practices: Avoiding contaminated food and drinking clean water.
  • Antibiotics & Antivirals: Used for bacterial and some viral infections.

Market Insights

The infectious disease market encompasses diagnostics, therapeutics, and preventive measures aimed at identifying, treating, and preventing diseases caused by pathogens such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, and parasites.

Market Segments and Growth Projections

  • Infectious Disease Diagnostics: Valued at USD 22.94 billion in 2023, projected to reach USD 43.09 billion by 2031.
  • Infectious Disease Therapeutics: Estimated at USD 123.91 billion in 2025, anticipated to reach USD 179.91 billion by 2034.
  • Molecular Diagnostics: Valued at USD 11.88 billion in 2023, expected to surpass USD 26.27 billion by 2032.

Key Market Drivers

  • Technological advancements in diagnostics.
  • Rising prevalence of infectious diseases.
  • Increased healthcare expenditure.
