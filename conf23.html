Introduction to Obesity Conference

Join us for the Obesity Conference 2025, a Webinar scheduled for September 11–12, 2025, focused on combating obesity through research, clinical practice, and innovation. This online gathering will connect leading obesity specialists, endocrinologists, dietitians, surgeons, researchers, and public health professionals to discuss the latest trends in obesity prevention, treatment, and long-term management. Hosted by Research Summit LTD, the webinar centres around the theme “Innovating Solutions for Obesity Care.” The program features interactive keynote talks, expert discussions, case-based sessions, and virtual networking opportunities—offering participants the chance to engage with global leaders and shape the future of obesity care, from anywhere in the world .

Key Areas of Obesity

  • Causes & Risk Factors: Obesity is influenced by genetics, poor diet, sedentary lifestyle, and metabolic disorders, increasing risks of diabetes, heart disease, and stroke.
  • Prevention & Management: Lifestyle modifications, balanced nutrition, regular exercise, medical interventions, and behavioral therapy help in weight control and long-term health.
  • Health Impacts & Complications: Obesity affects physical and mental well-being, leading to chronic illnesses, reduced mobility, and psychological distress.

Market Insights in Obesity<

The obesity treatment market is expanding rapidly due to escalating global obesity rates and rising health awareness. In 2024, the market was valued at approximately USD 20 billion, with forecasts indicating growth to over USD 40 billion by 2030. Factors such as increased adoption of anti-obesity medications, advancements in bariatric surgery, and digital wellness platforms are driving demand. North America holds a dominant market share, while emerging economies in Asia-Pacific are witnessing swift growth. Innovations in treatment strategies and public-private partnerships are further accelerating development. This trend underscores the urgent need for multidisciplinary approaches to address the obesity epidemic.

    Key Topics & Tracks

    Explore the latest advancements and research in Obesity through our comprehensive tracks:

    Obesity Management

    • Effective obesity management involves a multidisciplinary approach combining lifestyle modifications, medical interventions, behavioural therapy, and sometimes surgery. Tailored treatment plans help individuals achieve sustainable weight loss and improve metabolic health.

    Metabolic Syndrome

    • Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of conditions—high blood pressure, high blood sugar, excess abdominal fat, and abnormal cholesterol levels—that raise the risk for heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes, often linked to obesity.

    Childhood Obesity

    • Childhood obesity is a growing global concern, contributing to early onset of health issues like diabetes and hypertension. It is often influenced by genetics, lifestyle, and environmental factors, requiring early intervention.

    Bariatric Surgery

    • Bariatric surgery is a medical procedure designed for weight loss in severely obese patients. It alters the digestive system to restrict food intake or nutrient absorption, helping reduce obesity-related complications.

    Obesity and Diabetes

    • Obesity is a major risk factor for type 2 diabetes, primarily due to increased insulin resistance. Managing weight through diet, exercise, and medication can significantly lower diabetes risk and improve glucose control.

    Nutritional Therapy

    • Nutritional therapy uses personalized diet plans to address obesity. It emphasizes balanced nutrition, portion control, and nutrient-dense food choices, promoting gradual and sustainable weight loss while improving overall health.

    Anti-Obesity Drugs

    • Pharmacological treatments for obesity target appetite regulation, fat absorption, or metabolic rate. These medications support lifestyle changes and are typically prescribed for individuals with obesity-related health risks.

    Genetic Obesity Factors

    • Genetic predisposition plays a role in obesity by influencing appetite, metabolism, and fat storage. Understanding these genetic markers helps in developing personalized treatment and prevention strategies.

    Obesity and Cardiovascular Risk

    • Obesity significantly raises the risk of cardiovascular diseases by contributing to hypertension, high cholesterol, and atherosclerosis. Weight reduction improves heart health and lowers long-term cardiac risks.

    Behavioural Therapy

    • Behavioural strategies focus on modifying eating and activity habits through counselling, goal-setting, and self-monitoring. These interventions are key to long-term success in obesity treatment and relapse prevention.

    Obesity Prevention Strategies

    • Preventing obesity involves promoting healthy eating, regular physical activity, and public health policies that support active lifestyles. Early education and community support are vital to effective prevention.

    Endocrinology of Obesity

    • The endocrine system influences obesity through hormones like insulin, leptin, and ghrelin. Disruptions in hormonal balance can lead to increased fat storage and reduced energy expenditure.

    Public Health and Obesity

    • Public health efforts address obesity through awareness campaigns, policy changes, and improved access to healthy foods and exercise. Community-level initiatives are essential in reversing obesity trends.

    Obesity and Mental Health

    • There is a strong link between obesity and mental health conditions such as depression and anxiety. Addressing emotional well-being is a critical component of comprehensive obesity care.
