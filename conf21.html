Key Topics & Tracks

Explore the latest advancements and research in Osteoporosis & Orthopedic Surgery through our comprehensive tracks:

Osteoporosis

  • Osteoporosis is a metabolic bone disorder characterized by reduced bone mass and microarchitectural deterioration, leading to increased fracture risk. This topic explores current research, diagnostic methods, and preventive strategies aimed at minimizing complications, especially in aging populations.

Orthopaedic Surgery

  • Orthopaedic surgery focuses on diagnosing and treating musculoskeletal conditions through surgical interventions. This session will cover the latest advancements in surgical techniques, innovations in implant technology, and the integration of robotics in orthopaedic procedures.

Bone Mineral Density (BMD)

  • BMD testing is critical for assessing bone strength and diagnosing osteoporosis. This topic will discuss the latest imaging technologies, including DEXA scans, and their role in predicting fracture risk and monitoring treatment effectiveness.

Fragility Fractures

  • Fragility fractures occur from low-impact injuries and are common in individuals with weakened bones. This session will delve into prevention, early diagnosis, and multidisciplinary management strategies to reduce the burden of fractures in osteoporotic patients.

Arthroplasty

  • Arthroplasty involves replacing damaged joints, most commonly hips and knees, to restore mobility and relieve pain. Discussions will focus on surgical techniques, implant materials, patient outcomes, and advancements in personalized joint replacement.

Hip Fracture

  • Hip fractures are serious injuries often seen in the elderly, with significant morbidity. This topic covers surgical and non-surgical treatment options, postoperative care, and strategies for preventing future fractures through early osteoporosis management.

Knee Replacement

  • Knee replacement surgery is a common procedure for severe arthritis and joint damage. Presentations will highlight surgical innovations, rehabilitation protocols, and outcomes that improve mobility and patient quality of life.

Minimally Invasive Orthopaedic Surgery

  • Minimally invasive techniques reduce surgical trauma and recovery time. This session will feature updates on arthroscopy, image-guided procedures, and small-incision surgeries that optimize patient outcomes and reduce hospital stays.

Spine Surgery

  • Spine surgery addresses conditions such as herniated discs, spinal stenosis, and vertebral fractures. The focus will be on new surgical approaches, spinal implants, and postoperative rehabilitation for improved spinal alignment and mobility.

Robotic-Assisted Orthopaedic Surgery

  • Robotic systems in orthopaedic surgery enhance precision, planning, and outcomes. This topic will cover the integration of robotics in joint replacements and spine surgeries, along with the benefits for both surgeons and patients.

Orthopaedic Implants and Prosthetics

  • Orthopaedic implants restore function in damaged joints and bones. Topics will include innovations in biomaterials, prosthetic design, infection prevention, and long-term durability in both trauma and degenerative cases.

Geriatric Bone Health

  • As life expectancy rises, maintaining bone health in the elderly is crucial. This session explores age-related bone loss, fall prevention, and comprehensive approaches to managing musculoskeletal health in older adults.

Osteoarthritis Surgery

  • Surgical intervention becomes necessary when osteoarthritis severely impairs function. Discussions will focus on indications for surgery, joint preservation techniques, and postoperative outcomes in hip, knee, and hand osteoarthritis.

Orthopaedic Trauma Management

  • Managing traumatic injuries to bones and joints requires rapid diagnosis and intervention. This session will cover fracture stabilization techniques, emergency orthopedic care, and advances in trauma implant systems.
